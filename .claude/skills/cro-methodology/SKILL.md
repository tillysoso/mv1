# CRO Methodology

A systematic, evidence-based approach to conversion rate optimization. **Don't guess — discover.** Research why visitors don't convert before making changes.

## Core Principle

Conversion Rate Experts' approach: understand visitor objections through research, then systematically counter them with evidence-backed persuasion at the exact friction points.

## The 9-Step CRO Process

1. **Define success metrics** — what does "convert" mean? (purchase, sign-up, demo booked)
2. **Audit current performance** — where in the funnel do users drop off?
3. **Customer research** — discover real objections (surveys, interviews, support tickets)
4. **Inventory persuasion assets** — testimonials, awards, credentials, guarantees
5. **Map objections to counter-objections** — O/CO Framework
6. **Prioritize opportunities** — ICE score (Impact, Confidence, Ease)
7. **Hypothesis design** — structured experiment hypotheses
8. **A/B test** — controlled experiments with statistical rigor
9. **Scale wins** — propagate winning patterns organization-wide

## Customer Research Methods

Where to find real objections (not assumptions):

- **Exit surveys**: "What almost stopped you from completing this?" (Hotjar, Qualaroo)
- **Post-purchase surveys**: "What was your biggest concern before buying?"
- **Support tickets**: Mine for repeated objections
- **Sales call recordings**: What does the sales team hear?
- **Review sites**: What do negative reviews say?
- **User sessions**: Hotjar, FullStory recordings of drop-off moments

## The O/CO Framework

For every **Objection**, place a specific **Counter-Objection** at the moment of friction.

| Objection | Counter-Objection | Placement |
|-----------|------------------|-----------|
| "Is this secure?" | Security badges, SSL indicators | Near payment form |
| "Will it work for me?" | Case studies matching their profile | Near CTA |
| "What if I don't like it?" | Money-back guarantee, free trial | Near pricing |
| "Is this company legit?" | Press logos, customer count | Above fold |

## Persuasion Assets Inventory

Systematically catalog and deploy proof:

- **Social proof**: testimonials, reviews, user counts, logos
- **Authority**: press coverage, awards, certifications, expert endorsements
- **Guarantees**: money-back, free trial, satisfaction promise
- **Scarcity/urgency**: limited availability, time-sensitive offers (use only when true)
- **Specificity**: exact numbers beat vague claims ("saved 4.2 hours/week" > "saves time")

## Hypothesis Design

Structure: "If we [change X], then [metric Y] will improve because [research-backed reason from evidence Z]."

Example: "If we add a 'No credit card required' note near the sign-up CTA, then sign-up rate will improve because exit surveys show 31% of visitors cited payment concern as their reason for leaving."

## ICE Prioritization

Score each test idea 1–10 on:
- **Impact**: How much will it move the metric if it works?
- **Confidence**: How sure are we it will work? (based on research)
- **Ease**: How easy is it to implement and test?

ICE Score = (Impact + Confidence + Ease) / 3. Test highest scores first.

## A/B Testing Standards

- **Single variable**: change only one thing per test
- **Sample size**: calculate before starting (use a significance calculator)
- **Duration**: run for at least one full business cycle (typically 2–4 weeks)
- **Significance**: 95% statistical confidence before calling a winner
- **Segment results**: overall winner may not win for mobile, new vs. returning, etc.

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Testing ideas without research | Research objections first |
| Copying competitor changes | Your audience ≠ their audience |
| Stopping tests too early | Wait for statistical significance |
| Only testing above the fold | Objections occur throughout the page |
| Using generic "best practices" | Discover your audience's specific barriers |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No → Action |
|----------|----------------|
| Do you know the #1 reason visitors don't convert? | Run an exit survey this week |
| Have you mapped every objection on your key pages? | Interview 5 non-converting visitors |
| Are persuasion assets placed at friction points? | Audit placement against objection map |
| Are test hypotheses based on research? | Require research citation for every hypothesis |
| Are you waiting for statistical significance? | Set rules before starting; don't peek early |
