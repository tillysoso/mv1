# StoryBrand Messaging Framework

Clarify brand messaging by making the **customer the hero** — not the brand. Customers don't buy products they don't understand. If you confuse, you lose.

## Core Principle

"Customers don't buy the best products — they buy the ones they can understand the fastest."

The brand is the **Guide** (Yoda, Gandalf). The customer is the **Hero** (Luke, Frodo). Your job is to help them win, not to tell your story.

## The SB7 Framework

Every effective brand story has seven elements:

### 1. A Character (The Hero)

Define the **specific desire** of your customer — not everything they want, just the one thing related to your product.

Bad: "Our customers want to grow their business"
Good: "Our customers want to close more deals without spending weekends on admin"

### 2. Has a Problem

Three levels of problem:

| Level | Type | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| **External** | The surface problem | "My email takes 3 hours a day" |
| **Internal** | The emotional frustration | "I feel like I'm failing at work" |
| **Philosophical** | The injustice | "My time should be spent on real work" |

**Personify the villain** — the external villain creates the problem (inbox overload, complexity, manual processes). Name it. Attack it. Never the customer, always the villain.

Internal problems drive purchases more than external ones. Address both.

### 3. And Meets a Guide

Position your brand as the guide with two traits only:

- **Empathy**: "We understand how frustrating [problem] is"
- **Authority**: 3 forms of evidence (testimonials, statistics, logos, awards)

Never claim to be the hero. Never say "we're the best" — show it through evidence.

### 4. Who Gives Them a Plan

Customers don't take action without a clear path. Provide 3–4 simple steps.

Two plan types:
- **Process plan**: Steps to becoming a customer ("Sign up → Connect your tools → Automate your first workflow")
- **Agreement plan**: Principles that remove purchase anxiety ("We never sell your data. We offer a 30-day guarantee. You can cancel anytime.")

### 5. And Calls Them to Action

Every piece of communication needs a **direct CTA**. Customers don't act without being asked.

- **Direct CTA**: "Buy now", "Book a demo", "Start free trial"
- **Transitional CTA**: Lower commitment action for not-ready visitors — "Download guide", "Watch demo", "See pricing"

Both should be visible at every decision point.

### 6. That Helps Them Avoid Failure

Show what's at stake if they don't act. Paint realistic consequences of inaction.

People are more motivated by avoiding loss than gaining something. What does failure look like for your customer?

### 7. And Ends in Success

Paint the **transformation** — what life looks like after they win with your help.

Be specific. "Save time" is weak. "Spend Friday afternoons with your family instead of catching up on email" is strong.

## The One-Liner

Formula: **"We help [CHARACTER] who struggle with [PROBLEM] to [SOLUTION] so they can [RESULT]."**

Example: "We help small business owners who struggle with overwhelming email to automate their inbox so they can focus on growing their business."

Test: Can a stranger understand exactly what you do and who you serve in 5 seconds?

## BrandScript to Copy

Before writing any copy, fill in:
- Hero: [specific customer and their specific desire]
- Problem: [external] / [internal] / [philosophical]
- Guide: [empathy statement] + [3 authority proofs]
- Plan: [3–4 steps to working with you]
- CTA: [direct] / [transitional]
- Failure: [specific consequence of not acting]
- Success: [specific transformation after winning]

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Making your brand the hero | Make the customer the hero; you're the guide |
| Talking about your history/values on the homepage | Lead with customer problem, not company story |
| Vague success statements | Get specific: what does their life look like after? |
| No direct CTA | Add a clear, specific ask at every decision point |
| Listing features instead of outcomes | Translate every feature into a customer benefit |

## Quick Diagnostic

| Question | If No → Action |
|----------|----------------|
| Is the homepage about the customer, not the company? | Rewrite opening lines from customer's perspective |
| Is there a clear direct CTA above the fold? | Add "Start free trial" or "Book demo" button |
| Can a stranger understand the value in 5 seconds? | Read it to someone unfamiliar — watch for confusion |
| Does the copy address the internal problem? | Add empathy statement addressing the emotion |
| Is there a specific vision of success painted? | Describe one specific moment of transformation |
